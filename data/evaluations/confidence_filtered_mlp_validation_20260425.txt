Key finding:
  Confidence filtering improves validation top-1 policy accuracy from about 4-5% to about 10-13%.
  This supports the hypothesis that very flat teacher policies are harmful training targets.
  However, the model still overfits: training accuracy keeps rising to 79.37%, while validation accuracy remains low and validation policy loss rises from 3.08 to 4.58.

Interpretation:
  Filtering clear teacher positions helps, but is not sufficient to produce strong generalization by itself.
  The filtered dataset is much smaller, so overfitting becomes easier.
  A candidate trained only on these samples would likely need regularization, early stopping, or additional high-confidence data.

Conclusion:
  min_policy_confidence is a useful diagnostic/training control and should remain available.
  Do not promote or rely on a confidence-filtered model yet.

Recommended next steps:
  1. Try soft/moderately sharpened targets on filtered samples, e.g. --policy_temperature 0.25 with --min_policy_confidence 0.3.
  2. Generate more high-confidence teacher data, preferably with more simulations or lower move-selection temperature.
  3. Add sample weighting by confidence instead of hard filtering, to avoid throwing away too much data.
  4. Consider evaluating early checkpoints around the best validation accuracy, but only as a diagnostic, not promotion.
