AlphaZero One-Hot MLP Validation Diagnostic
Date: 2026-04-25

Goal:
  Check whether longer one-hot MLP training improves generalization or only memorizes the training set.

Code changes:
  train/dataset.py:
    Added create_dataloaders with train/validation split support.

  train/train.py:
    Added --val_split and --split_seed.
    Training output now reports TrainPolicyAcc / TrainValueMAE / TrainValueSignAcc.
    When validation split is enabled, output also reports ValLoss / ValPolicy / ValValue / ValPolicyAcc / ValValueMAE / ValValueSignAcc.

Key finding:
  Training policy top-1 accuracy rises steadily from 5.8% to 25.18%.
  Validation policy top-1 accuracy stays around 4-5% and does not improve.
  Training policy loss falls from 3.6192 to 2.8515.
  Validation policy loss rises from 3.6525 to 3.9980 after early epochs.

Interpretation:
  The MLP is memorizing one-hot teacher labels from the training split instead of learning a policy that generalizes to held-out self-play positions.
  This explains why the long one-hot model had better training PolicyAcc but worse arena strength than the 50-epoch one-hot model.
  Lower training loss is not a reliable checkpoint-selection criterion for this setup.

Conclusion:
  Stop selecting MLP checkpoints by train loss alone.
  Do not simply train one-hot MLP longer.
  The current MLP + one-hot imitation pipeline overfits quickly and generalizes poorly.

Recommended next steps:
  1. Use validation metrics when deciding whether a model is worth arena evaluation.
  2. Evaluate earlier checkpoints by arena, because best play strength may occur before train overfit deepens.
  3. Train the existing CNN architecture with the same validation split to check whether spatial inductive bias improves validation PolicyAcc.
  4. If CNN generalizes better, consider either using CNN for training/evaluation research or later adding CNN inference support.
