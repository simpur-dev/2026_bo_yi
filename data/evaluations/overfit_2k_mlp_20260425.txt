AlphaZero MLP Overfit Sanity Check
Date: 2026-04-25

Goal:
  Use only 2000 samples and train for 200 epochs to check whether training-set policy top-1 accuracy can approach 60%+.

Code changes for diagnostics:
  train/dataset.py supports max_samples.
  train/train.py reports PolicyAcc, ValueMAE, and ValueSignAcc.

Command:

Conclusion:
  Overall PolicyAcc did not approach 60%; it only reached 22.8%.
  However, this does not mean the MLP cannot learn the training data. The teacher policy targets are usually very flat: the average best-action probability is only 0.1646 and only 6.15% of samples have target_max >= 0.6.
  On sharp/decisive samples, the model memorizes the teacher top action almost perfectly.

Interpretation:
  Overall top-1 policy accuracy is a misleading primary metric for this dataset because most MCTS visit distributions are broad and ambiguous.
  The model fits value targets very well (ValueSignAcc 98.1%, ValueMAE 0.0573), and it fits decisive policy targets well.
  Candidate failure is therefore more likely caused by weak/flat policy targets, ambiguous early-game data, or mismatch between soft imitation loss and actual play strength, rather than a basic inability to train.

Recommended next step:
  Add better training diagnostics such as target entropy, policy KL/cross-entropy gap, and sharp-policy top-1 accuracy.
  Consider training/evaluating on sharper targets: lower self-play temperature, stronger visit-count sharpening, or weighting decisive samples more heavily.
